Maui Permit Checklists

Published and maintained by Oʻen Maui Architecture + Design

These are the permit checklists we use in our own practice — cleaned up, cross-referenced, and published for anyone on the island who needs them. Homeowners, contractors, other design firms, or just people trying to understand the process: you're welcome to use them.

The goal is simple: take something that is already public but scattered across county and state websites and put it in one clear place. If it saves someone a trip to the counter at the DSA or a phone call to DLNR, that's a win.

Please read before using this checklist

These checklists are educational summaries of publicly available Maui County and State of Hawaiʻi permitting requirements. They are published by Oʻen Maui Architecture + Design as a community resource and are provided as-is, without warranty of any kind.

They are not legal advice, engineering advice, or official regulatory guidance. They are not a substitute for the current requirements published by Maui County (Department of Public Works, Department of Planning, SHPD), the State of Hawaiʻi (DLNR, DOH), or any other agency with jurisdiction over your project.

Rules change. We update these documents on a quarterly schedule, and every update is recorded with a date and a change log. Before you rely on any item for a submission, confirm it against the current published requirement from the relevant agency.

If you are unsure whether an item applies to your project, talk to a licensed professional or the permitting agency directly. If you find an error or something out of date, please let us know through GitHub Discussions or email us directly.

Heads up — new rules effective July 1, 2026

Three Hawaiʻi laws passed in 2025 change how housing permits and historic-preservation review work in Maui County. One of them, Act 295, creates a new expedited permit pathway for qualifying single-family and multi-family housing projects starting July 1, 2026. It does not apply to every project, and the details matter.

We have written a plain-language summary of what is actually in the laws — and what is not — so you can tell the difference between the rumor version and the rule version. Read our July 2026 permit reform notes →
